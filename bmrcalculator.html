BMR Calculator

The main principal in fulfilling energy needs is actually simple; it’s all about balance, because if you consume more calories than needed, it can cause an increase in body weight in the near future aside of increasing the risk to suffer from certain diseases, especially degenerative diseases. If you consume less calories than needed, you’ll encounter weight loss but at the same time decreasing the function of bodily organs due to the deficiency of required amount of energy.

Harris-Benedict Formula

With the help of your body weight, height, age and gender you can determine your daily calorie requirement with the Harris-Benedict formula. This formula is commonly used by nutritionists, it consists of the resting metabolism plus the energy you need for all your activities.

The above calculation shows your average basal energy expenditure (BEE), also known as basal metabolic rate (BMR). The average BEE for American women is about 1,400 calories while for men it is roughly 1,800. Your BEE is an estimate of the energy your body uses at rest. Typically about 60% of our total energy needs to come from resting metabolism, but this varies greatly depending on our activity level. In the calculation for total daily calorie needs, we need to take into account the level of daily activity, whereby the BMR will be multiplied by a number determined by your level of activity.
